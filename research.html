Image-Guided Navigated Interventions

Our research focuses on using advanced imaging and navigation systems (ultrasound, EM trackers, navigation displays) to improve the accuracy of medical procedures in cardiology, breast cancer, radiation therapy, and virtual reality, leading to better patient outcomes.

Training and Skill Assessment for Image-Guided Interventions

Medical education has shifted to competency-based learning. Our lab collaborates with the Faculty of Health Sciences to develop simulation-based curricula using EM trackers, ultrasound phantoms, and surgical simulators. This approach improves student skill proficiency and has earned international recognition for advancing medical education.

Machine and Deep Learning Applications in Medical Imaging

Artificial intelligence transforms medical imaging by quickly analyzing ultrasound, MRI, and CT scans to detect subtle abnormalities for early disease detection. We develop AI solutions and integrate them into procedural guidance and training systems to enhance healthcare diagnostics.
