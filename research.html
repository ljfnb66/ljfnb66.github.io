Research Themes

Image-Guided Navigated Interventions

Our research focuses on using advanced imaging and navigation systems to improve the accuracy of medical procedures across fields like cardiology, breast cancer, radiation therapy, and virtual reality, leading to better patient outcomes. We integrate technologies like ultrasound machines, position trackers, and navigation displays to enhance procedural precision.

Training and Skill Assessment for Image-Guided Interventions

Medical education has shifted to competency-based learning. Our lab collaborates with the Faculty of Health Sciences to develop simulation-based curricula using tools like EM trackers, ultrasound phantoms, and surgical simulators. This improves student skill proficiency and has earned international recognition for advancing medical education through technology-integrated training systems.

Machine Learning in Medical Imaging

Leveraging AI to analyze medical images (MRI, CT, ultrasound) for early disease detection. Our work includes developing deep learning models to identify subtle abnormalities, integrating these into clinical workflows, and creating AI-augmented training systems to enhance diagnostic skill development in medical professionals.
